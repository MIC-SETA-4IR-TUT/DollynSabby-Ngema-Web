HOW WE BEGAN

Dolly & Sabby's Farm began with a husband and wife, R102 Ndondakusuka Rd, a Mangethe pasture and a commitment to animal welfare. Sabby Ngema and Dolly Ngema aspired to produce ethical food and a sustainable business. Instead, they built a transformational one. Today Dolly & Sabby's Farm partners with over 275 small family farms across the South African country. Every hen is humanely treated, every egg is pasture-raised and we continue to elevate our (and the industry’s) standards, continuing Dolly and Sabby’s commitment to conscious capitalism.

OUR FARMERS

When it comes to producing food ethically, Dolly & Sally's farmers are outstanding in their fields and pastures. We’re proud to work with people as committed to animal welfare and ethical eating as we are. We support them with fair pay, in addition to resources and expert advice from our dedicated Farmer Support Team.

Dolly Ngema

Armed with a mechanical engineering degree, Dolly never saw herself pursuing agriculture, a common trade in her hometown, but after exploring the food industry and learning more about food systems, she and her husband began to look into ways they could play a role in making farming better. Seven years later, Dolly and her family now own a farm and have been farming for a little over a two years. Her husband, a physical therapy assistant, their parents and their two kids all play a role in taking care of the girls. From cleaning out the egg room, to everyday upkeep.

Sabby Ngema

Sabby’s enthusiasm and dedication to his girls is a sight to see. To Sabby, embracing the farming lifestyle and raising pasture-raised hens is just an extension of his belief to treat animals right and eat natural.
